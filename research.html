Research

Published

  • Marvel Rivals Toxicity (in progress): Investigating toxicity patterns and community impacts within Marvel Rivals, aiming to develop broader insights into online gaming behavior and moderation.

Personal / School Projects

  • The Role of Fitbit in Digital Health and Privacy: Analyzed Fitbit as a digital health tool—examining usage outcomes before and after Google’s acquisition, with a focus on data privacy implications.
  • Beyond Anti-Cheat: Social Engineering in Gaming Security: Explored how social engineering tactics compromise gaming environments, challenging the assumption that anti-cheat tools alone can secure online play.
  • Centralization ≠ Immunity: Rethinking Blind Trust in Online Gaming Networks: Critiqued the belief that centralized server infrastructures inherently protect players from attacks, using examples from major gaming platforms.
  • Myth: “Just Buy the Right Security Tool and You’ll Be Safe”: Debunked the misconception that purchasing the latest security software guarantees protection, highlighting the human and process factors often overlooked.
  • Theoretical Framework for Black Hole Creation in the LHC: A mini group analysis outlining the physics behind hypothetical black hole formation in the Large Hadron Collider, including safety considerations.
  • Unraveling the Essence of “Mannen Bridge”: A formal art-historical analysis of Utagawa Hiroshige’s woodblock print “Mannen Bridge in Fukagawa District, no. 51,” examining symbolic choices and narrative elements.
